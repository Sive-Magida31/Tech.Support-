Overview of Escalation Levels

Our escalation process ensures that each support case is handled by the appropriate team based on complexity and urgency. Below is the step-by-step guide for escalating support requests from Front Line Support to Second and Third Level Support.

Front Line Support

Handles initial inquiries and basic troubleshooting. Escalate if:

  • Issue Complexity: Beyond agentâ€™s knowledge or expertise.
  • Repetitive Issues: Reported multiple times without resolution.
  • Time Constraints: Unresolved within SLA time frame.
  • Customer Request: Customer requests higher-level support.

Second Level Support

Handles complex issues. Escalate if:

  • Technical Diagnostics: Requires log analysis or system diagnostics.
  • Configuration Changes: Involves significant configuration changes.
  • System Integration: Involves multiple systems needing coordination.

Third Level Support

The highest support level. Escalate if:

  • Critical System Failures: Major system outage or business impact.
  • Newly Discovered Bugs: Appears to be a new software bug.
  • Vendor Involvement: Requires vendor support or collaboration.
